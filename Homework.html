Homemade Yougurt

            Total:          15 mins
            Prep:           10 mins 
            Cook:            5 mins
            Waiting Time:    8 hrs
            Yield:           1 quart (4 servings)
Yougurt is fairly easy to make at home and the result is much better in flavor and consistency than most commercial yougurt. You'll and also be able to choose what does or doesn't go into your yougurt (commercial brands often include thickener and other non-dalry ingredients)

Ingredients

  • 1 quart/ 1 litter milk
  • 2 tablespoons yougurt (with actve cultures: all commercial brands will stare on the container if the yougurtt contains active culture)

Step To Make It

  • Gently heat the milk over medium low heat until it reaches 180oF/82oC. Turn off the heat and the milk cool down to between 106oF/41oC and 110oF/43oC.
  • While the heated milk is cooling, wash the jars and leave them filled with hot water so the glass will be warm when you add the yogurt.
  • When the milk has cooled enough, warm a large bowl or measuring cup by faling it with hot water Empty out the water. Straln rhe milk through a fine mesh strainer Into the warmed bowl. Whisk in the yogurt. Paur out the water in the jars and pour in the milk plus yogurt mixture. Fasten the lids
  • Place the jars in a warm but not hot place where they can remain undisturbed for B hours or overnight, An old fashioned oven in which the pilot light is always on is perfect.
